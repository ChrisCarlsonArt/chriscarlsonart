Sometimes when you're feeling creative, the most difficult part can be thinking of what to draw. I want to save you some time and jump start your creativity, so I've put together a list of easy sidewalk chalk art ideas for you. Each idea includes a description and a couple of images for inspiration. You can right click on any of these images to save them, then print them out as references while you draw. This isn't cheating; many artists use reference photos to help them create their artwork. Now you can get out there and start chalking faster!

## 1. Animals

Animals are one of the best subjects for chalk art. We see them everywhere and they come in all shapes and sizes. Many of us even have animals living in our houses! Here are some of the best animals for chalk drawings:

### Dogs

Dogs can be a great inspiration for artwork. They have so many different appearances and expressions that the possibilities are endless. I recommend using a cartoon style for a cute and fun drawing, or if you're a more experienced artist you can use a realistic style for a more refined drawing.

## 2. Vehicles

Vehicles like cars, trucks, and trains are a big part of our everyday lives. It's only natural that we would want to draw them. There is a huge variety of shapes, sizes, and colors found in vehicles, so you can get creative with your drawings! I recommend drawing white lines coming off the back of your vehicle to show direction and speed. You can also use your fingers to blur the back half of a vehicle to make it look extra fast.

## 3. Flowers

Flowers can be one of the most fun things to draw with chalk. The color possibilities are endless! You can invent your own flower or try to draw one that really exists. I recommend using multiple colors to create your flowers. Instead of just drawing a yellow flower, add more orange to the ends of the petals. You can blend the colors together using your fingers to create a gradient. This will make your drawing look extra vibrant!

## 4. Dinosaurs

Dinosaurs are perfect for chalk art because they can be any color you want. You can go with a traditional green dino, or get creative with a purple and pink version! I recommend using the dinosaur's mouth and eyes to convey emotion. Big round eyes and a smiling mouth will make your dino look happy and friendly, while squinted eyes and a snarling mouth will make your creature look scary.

## 5. Interactive Designs

Interactive designs can be some of the most fun things to create with chalk. These are designs that you can pose with, so it looks like you are interacting with the artwork! Once you start interacting with your artwork you will notice the creative floodgates open and new ideas start pouring out.

### Wings

Wings are a really fun and easy interactive chalk art design. You can make butterfly wings, dragon wings, angel wings, even invent your own custom wings! Just leave a small space in between the two wings for a person to pose. When you are finished drawing, have someone lay down in between the two wings, then take a photo standing above them. Now they have wings!

### Balloons

Balloons are one of the simplest things you can draw with chalk. They have a very easy shape and come in all kinds of bright colors. I recommend drawing a bright white highlight near the top of the balloon. This will make it look like the sun is reflecting off the material. Then draw strings hanging down below the balloons. When someone lays on the ground and pretends to hold the strings it will look like they are floating! Take a photo from directly above the artwork to get the best effect.

### Comic Book Speech Bubble

The comic book speech bubble is the quickest and easiest idea on this list. You can draw out the bubble in seconds and then have a blast thinking of things to write inside. Just have someone lay down next to the bubble and it looks like they are in a comic book!

## Get out there and make some chalk art!

Now that you have some inspiration, it's time to get out there and make some chalk art. Remember that chalk art is supposed to be fun, so don't put any pressure on yourself. Just draw things that make you feel happy or excited. Experiment with new techniques and materials. Be silly! Most importantly, connect with friends and family while having fun and making art.
